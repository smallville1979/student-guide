💸 The Biggest Struggle

FOOD
COSTS

Food prices have shot up. Here's how students across the UK are actually beating the system.

01
Plan your meals weeklyWrite a meal plan before you shop. You only buy what you need — no waste, no last-minute takeaways.
02
Shop at Aldi firstAldi is consistently cheapest for UK students. Do your main shop there, top up at Tesco only when needed.
03
Never shop hungrySounds obvious — it works. Shopping on a full stomach means you stick to the list and resist temptation.
04
Cook from scratchBatch cooking saves both time and money. Rice, pasta, lentils, eggs — cheap, filling, and flexible.
05
Best days: Sun & MonQuieter stores + more yellow-sticker reduced items. Sunday evenings especially good for bargains.
06
Make your own lunchA packed lunch saves £5–£8 a day. Over a uni term that's hundreds of pounds back in your pocket.

🏠 The Second Struggle

RENT &
HOUSING

Hidden fees, long summer contracts, and overpriced rooms catch students out every year. Here's what to watch for.

📋

Read EVERY clause

Landlords bury fees in contracts. Read every line before signing. Look for clauses about summer rent, cleaning charges, and early exit penalties.

🔎 Ask your uni housing team to check it
📅

Avoid Summer Lock-ins

Some contracts run 52 weeks — meaning you pay rent over summer even if you're home. Hunt for 40–44 week contracts instead.

⚠️ Always check contract length
🔍

Start Looking Early

The best student houses go fast — often before Christmas for the following September. Don't leave it until March or you'll be stuck with what's left.

🗓️ Start in October/November
🏫

Use Your Uni Housing Team

Most universities offer free contract checking. They know local landlords and local scams. Always use this service before signing anything.

✅ Totally free at most unis
👥 The Third Struggle

SOCIAL
LIFE

Making friends at uni is harder than the brochures make it look. Here's what actually works.

🎯

Join Societies

Societies are the single best way to meet people outside your flat. Sports teams, hobby clubs, volunteering — shared interest makes conversation instant and natural.

💡 Join in week 1, even if nervous
⏳

Be Patient

Friendships take time. Most students feel lonely at some point in first year. That's normal. Keep showing up and your people will appear.

🙏 It genuinely gets better
📚

Talk to Course Mates

Invite a course mate for a coffee or library session. You already have something in common — use it. These friendships often outlast uni.

☕ Low stakes, high reward
