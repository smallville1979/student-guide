🏠 The Second Struggle

RENT &
HOUSING

Hidden fees, long summer contracts, and overpriced rooms catch students out every year. Here's what to watch for.

📋

Read EVERY Clause

Landlords bury fees in contracts. Read every line before signing — look for summer rent clauses, cleaning charges, and early exit penalties.

🔎 Ask your uni housing team to check it
📅

Avoid Summer Lock-ins

Some contracts run 52 weeks — meaning you pay rent over summer even if you're home. Hunt for 40–44 week contracts instead.

⚠️ Always check contract length
🔍

Start Looking Early

The best student houses go fast — often before Christmas for the following September. Don't leave it until March or you'll be stuck with what's left.

🗓️ Start in October/November
🏫

Use Your Uni Housing Team

Most universities offer free contract checking. They know local landlords and local scams. Always use this service before signing anything.

✅ Totally free at most unis
💳 Money Basics — No Judgement

MONEY
101

Nobody teaches us this stuff in school. Here's what overdrafts, credit cards, Plum, and Zilch actually are — explained simply, no jargon.

📖 What Is An Overdraft?

OVERDRAFTS EXPLAINED

An overdraft lets you spend more money than you actually have in your bank account — your balance goes below £0. Think of it like a short-term loan from your bank.

Student overdrafts are special — most UK banks (like Lloyds, Santander, HSBC) offer 0% interest overdrafts for students, meaning you can borrow up to £1,000–£3,000 and pay nothing extra back, as long as you repay it when you graduate. This is genuinely useful for emergencies — but treat it as a safety net, not spending money. Once you leave uni, the 0% period ends and interest kicks in fast, so pay it back as quickly as possible after graduation.

📖 What Is A Credit Card?

CREDIT CARDS EXPLAINED

A credit card lets you buy things now and pay for them later — usually at the end of the month. The card company pays the shop, then you pay the card company back.

Used right, they're brilliant: you get consumer protections on purchases, cashback rewards, and can build your credit score (which matters for renting and mortgages later).

Used wrong, they're dangerous: if you don't pay the full balance every month, interest charges of 20–30% stack up fast. The golden rule — only spend on a credit card what you already have in your bank account, and pay it off in full each month. Never just pay the minimum.

💡 Top Credit Trick

BUILD YOUR CREDIT SCORE NOW

Your credit score affects whether you can rent a flat, get a phone contract, or get a mortgage one day. As a student you can start building it for free right now.

Use a 0% student credit card for small regular purchases (like your weekly shop), then pay it off in full every single month. You build credit history, pay zero interest, and sometimes earn cashback. Register on the electoral roll at your uni address too — this alone boosts your score significantly. Check your score free at Experian, Clearscore, or Credit Karma.

Avoid Payday Loans

Payday loans charge outrageous interest — sometimes 1,500% APR. They're a debt trap. If you're in a cash crisis, your bank's 0% student overdraft, your uni hardship fund, or Citizens Advice are all better options first.

⚡ Secret Energy Tricks

GAS &
ELECTRIC

Most students and renters don't know these tricks exist. They do. Use them — they're free money.

🎟️

CAB Fuel Vouchers

Citizens Advice Bureau can provide fuel vouchers to help with gas and electric bills — and you can ask up to 3 times within 6 months. Most people have no idea this exists. Go to your local CAB office or call them and ask specifically about fuel vouchers.

💡 3 times in 6 months — FREE
🔋

Emergency Credit From Your Supplier

This is a secret almost nobody knows — if you're on a prepayment meter and can't top up, you can call your energy supplier and ask for emergency credit. Every major supplier offers it. They add credit to your meter immediately. You pay it back gradually when you next top up. Never go cold — just call and ask.

📞 Call your supplier and ask directly
🌡️

Warm Home Discount

If you're a student on a low income or qualifying benefit, you may be entitled to £150 off your energy bill through the Warm Home Discount scheme. Check eligibility via your energy supplier's website — it's automatic for some, but others need to apply.

💷 £150 off — worth checking
☎️

Call Before You Struggle

If you're falling behind on energy bills, call your supplier before it becomes a crisis. All major suppliers are legally required to offer payment plans, debt support, and hardship funds. Calling early means more options — waiting makes it worse.

🤝 They must help — it's the law

🧘 Feeling Overwhelmed?

ANXIETY &
STRESS

Stress and anxiety are incredibly common at uni. You're not broken — you're human. Here are real tools that help.

🕯️ SELF-PROTECTION RITUAL — 2 MINUTE RESET

When anxiety hits and your mind is racing, try this simple grounding practice.

1. Find somewhere quiet. Close your eyes.
2. Place your hand on your heart. Feel your heartbeat.
3. Take 3 slow deep breaths — in for 4 counts, hold for 4, out for 6.
4. Repeat quietly to yourself, as many times as you need:
"I am safe. I am protected. I am loved."

This grounding technique brings you back to the present moment and calms your nervous system. It works.

🌬️

Box Breathing

In for 4 counts, hold for 4, out for 4, hold for 4. Repeat 4 times. Used by the military under extreme pressure — works for exam nerves too.

⚡ Works in under 2 minutes
👋

5-4-3-2-1 Grounding

Name 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you can hear, 2 you can smell, 1 you can taste. Instantly pulls you out of panic and into the present.

🧠 Classic grounding technique
🚶

Move Your Body

A 10-minute walk outside genuinely reduces cortisol levels. You don't need a gym. Fresh air is free and it works immediately.

🌿 Free and instant
😴

Protect Your Sleep

Everything feels worse when you're exhausted. Anxiety, low mood, and poor focus are all amplified by bad sleep. Treat sleep like a revision session.

💤 7–9 hours is the target
✍️

Journal It Out

Write down what's worrying you. Getting thoughts out of your head and onto paper reduces their power. Even 5 minutes helps.

📓 No rules — just write
🗣️

Talk to Someone

Keeping it all inside makes anxiety worse. Tell a friend, family member, or counsellor how you're feeling. Your uni likely has free counselling — use it.

💬 You don't have to do this alone
📚 Exam Season

SURVIVING
EXAMS

Exam stress is real. Here's how to prep smart, manage the pressure, and actually remember what you've revised.

🗓️

Plan Backwards

Start from your exam date and work backwards. Block out revision time weekly — don't try to cram it all at the end.

🍅

Pomodoro Technique

25 minutes focused work, 5 minute break. Repeat. After 4 rounds, take a longer break. Prevents burnout and keeps focus sharp.

🧠

Active Recall

Don't just re-read notes. Test yourself. Flashcards, past papers, teaching it back to yourself — these actually work.

📵

Phone Away

One notification breaks focus for up to 23 minutes. Use Forest or Freedom app to block distractions.

💧

Stay Hydrated

Even mild dehydration reduces cognitive performance. Keep water on your desk during revision and in the exam hall.

🎧

Lo-Fi Focus Music

Background music without lyrics helps many students focus. YouTube has endless lo-fi study playlists.

📝

Past Papers Are Gold

The best revision is doing past papers under timed conditions. Mark your own work. Identify gaps. Repeat.

🤝

Study Groups

Teaching others locks in your own knowledge. Quiz each other, explain concepts out loud. Seriously underrated.

🚨 Exam Panic? Do This Right Now

If you're overwhelmed before an exam — stop. Close your eyes. Do 3 rounds of box breathing (in 4, hold 4, out 4, hold 4). Remind yourself: you have prepared. You know more than you think. One breath at a time.

You've done the workNerves are normalBreathe first, always
