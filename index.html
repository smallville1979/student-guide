💸 The Biggest Struggle

FOOD
COSTS

Food prices have shot up. Here's how students across the UK are actually beating the system.

01
Plan your meals weeklyWrite a meal plan before you shop. Buy only what you need — no waste, no last-minute takeaways.
02
Shop at Aldi firstAldi is consistently cheapest for UK students. Main shop there, small top-ups at Tesco only if needed.
03
Never shop hungryShopping on a full stomach means you stick to the list and avoid impulse buys. It genuinely works.
04
Cook from scratchBatch cooking saves both time and money. Rice, pasta, lentils, eggs — cheap, filling, and flexible.
05
Best days: Sun & MonQuieter stores and more yellow-sticker reduced items. Sunday evenings especially great for bargains.
06
Make your own lunchA packed lunch saves £5–£8 a day. Over a uni term that's hundreds of pounds back in your pocket.

🏠 The Second Struggle

RENT &
HOUSING

Hidden fees, long summer contracts, and overpriced rooms catch students out every year.

📋

Read EVERY clause

Landlords bury fees in contracts. Read every line before signing — look for clauses about summer rent, cleaning charges, and early exit penalties.

🔎 Ask your uni housing team to check it
📅

Avoid Summer Lock-ins

Some contracts run 52 weeks — meaning you pay rent over summer even if you're home. Hunt for 40–44 week contracts instead.

⚠️ Always check contract length
🔍

Start Looking Early

The best student houses go fast — often before Christmas for the following September. Don't leave it until March.

🗓️ Start in October/November
🏫

Use Your Uni Housing Team

Most universities offer free contract checking. They know local landlords and local scams. Always use this service before signing anything.

✅ Totally free at most unis

SOCIAL
LIFE

Making friends at uni is harder than the brochures make it look. Here's what actually works.

🎯

Join Societies

Societies are the single best way to meet people outside your flat. Sports teams, hobby clubs, volunteering — shared interest makes conversation instant and natural.

💡 Join in week 1, even if nervous

Friendships take time. Most students feel lonely at some point in first year. That's completely normal. Keep showing up and your people will appear.

🙏 It genuinely gets better

Talk to Course Mates

Invite a course mate for coffee or a library session. You already have something in common — use it. These friendships often outlast uni entirely.

☕ Low stakes, high reward
🧘 Feeling Overwhelmed?

ANXIETY &
STRESS

Stress and anxiety are incredibly common at uni. You're not broken — you're human. Here are real tools that help.

🕯️ SELF-PROTECTION RITUAL — 2 MINUTE RESET

When anxiety hits and your mind is racing, try this simple grounding practice.

1. Find somewhere quiet. Close your eyes.
2. Place your hand on your heart. Feel your heartbeat.
3. Take 3 slow deep breaths — in for 4 counts, hold for 4, out for 6.
4. Repeat quietly to yourself, as many times as you need:
"I am safe. I am protected. I am loved."

This grounding technique brings you back to the present moment and calms your nervous system.

🌬️

Box Breathing

In for 4 counts, hold for 4, out for 4, hold for 4. Repeat 4 times. Used by military and athletes under extreme pressure — works for exam nerves too.

⚡ Works in under 2 minutes
👋

5-4-3-2-1 Grounding

Name 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you can hear, 2 you can smell, 1 you can taste. Instantly pulls you out of panic and into the present moment.

🧠 Classic grounding technique
🚶

Move Your Body

A 10-minute walk outside genuinely reduces cortisol (stress hormone) levels. You don't need a gym. Fresh air is free and it works immediately.

🌿 Free and instant
😴

Protect Your Sleep

Everything feels worse when you're exhausted. Anxiety, low mood, and poor focus are all amplified by bad sleep. Treat sleep like a revision session.

💤 7–9 hours is the target
✍️

Journal It Out

Write down what's worrying you. Getting thoughts out of your head and onto paper reduces their power significantly. Even 5 minutes helps.

📓 No rules — just write
🗣️

Talk to Someone

Keeping it all inside makes anxiety worse. Tell a friend, family member, or counsellor how you're feeling. Your uni likely has free counselling — use it.

💬 You don't have to do this alone
📚 Exam Season

SURVIVING
EXAMS

Exam stress is real. Here's how to prep smart, manage the pressure, and actually remember what you've revised.

🗓️

Plan Backwards

Start from your exam date and work backwards. Block out revision time weekly — don't try to cram it all at the end.

🍅

Pomodoro Technique

25 minutes focused work, 5 minute break. Repeat. After 4 rounds, take a longer break. Prevents burnout and keeps focus sharp.

🧠

Active Recall

Don't just re-read notes. Test yourself. Flashcards, past papers, teaching it back to yourself — these actually work.

📵

Phone Away

One notification breaks your focus for up to 23 minutes. Put it in another room. Use Forest or Freedom app to block distractions.

💧

Stay Hydrated

Even mild dehydration reduces cognitive performance. Keep water on your desk during revision and in the exam hall.

🎧

Lo-Fi Focus Music

Background music without lyrics helps many students focus. YouTube has endless lo-fi study playlists — find what works for you.

📝

Past Papers Are Gold

The best revision is doing past papers under timed conditions. Mark your own work. Identify gaps. Repeat. Nothing beats this.

🤝

Study Groups

Teaching others locks in your own knowledge. Quiz each other, explain concepts out loud. Social revision is underrated.

🚨 Exam Panic? Do This Right Now

If you're overwhelmed before an exam — stop. Close your eyes. Do 3 rounds of box breathing (in 4, hold 4, out 4, hold 4). Remind yourself: you have prepared. You know more than you think. One breath at a time.

You've done the work Nerves are normal Breathe first, always
